oSTEM

I was the Event Coordinator for oSTEM. oSTEM is the national professional and collegiate organisation for queer people in stem. We work to provide professional development opportunities, solidarity, and community for LGBTQ+ workers in industry and academic settings. The Drexel chapter was started by Charlie Rose Stuart but folded due to the pandemic, with his help we have revitalised the organisation We have participated in Philly Code Fest, consulting with the CCI DEI Council, a professional headshots event with affirming workplace attire and makeup, serving as a guide for queer people in the workplace to feel comfortable with themselves.

TAing

I have a great interest in computer science education and have become a TA at Drexel to further
this. TAing has been an eye opening experience, letting me learn how to communicate ideas,
pedagogical technique, forming better relationships with professors, and reinforcing the concepts I learn. In this role, I also serve as a peer mentor for many younger students, some of which who've become TAs themself.

I have TAd for Introduction to CS and CS Principles, and Python I/II, but my main focus is the Freshman Design Sequence. As the Head TA for the Freshman Design Sequence, I work closely with the instructional team to refine pedegogical methods and refine the course. My changes, such as an increased emphasis on technical communication, clarified expectations, active learning, and TA training material and resources have had a noticeable impact through scores, student testimonials, and my 4-time nomination for the undergraduate TA award.
